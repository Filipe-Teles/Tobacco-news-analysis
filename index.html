Tobacco news analysis

In 2023 the Portuguese government wanted to implement a new law that restricted the sales of tobacco. But then the lobby came into force.

By Filipe Teles

Last year, the Portuguese government wanted to implement a law that restricted the selling of tobacco in vending machines and to forbid smoking in several open air spaces. What the government was not expecting was the interference of the tobacco industry lobby.

I collected all news pieces from the printing press - 31 - about the Tobacco Law from May to November, when the law was in discussion in Parliament, and analysed the coverage about the law.

This is the story of how the the Portuguese printing press covered the attempt of the government to enact the law that restricted the selling of tobacco. And how it let the tobacco industry and interest groups that revolve around it control the media narrative.

In 31 news pieces, the interest groups against the law are mentioned 36 times.

Phillip Morris, the tobacco company, owner of the Portuguese company Tabaqueira, is mentioned 13 times.

A study about the economic impact of the tobacco industry in Portugal, paid by Phillip Morris, is referenced seven times in these news pieces.

Those who were in favor of the law did not have the same luck.

The Portuguese Society of Pulmonology, the main defender of the law besides the government, is only mentioned twice in these 31 news pieces.

The Health minister and the secretary of the promotion of Health, the members of government responsible for designing the law, are mentioned 11 times.

The Portuguese Lung Foundation, one of the main organizations in the country that promotes respiratory health, is never mentioned.

In Portugal, according to the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation, tobacco killed more than 13 thousand Portuguese citizens in 2019.

This number was only mentioned once.

In the end, the law did not pass.
